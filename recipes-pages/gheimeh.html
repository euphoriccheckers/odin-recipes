Gheimeh

Description

Gheimeh is a comforting Persian stew cooked with beef and yellow split peas. This stew is super fragrant with melt-in-your-mouth meat cooked to perfection with earthy spices like cinnamon and turmeric. Like most Iranian stews, this stew is served with fluffy saffron rice.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 pound beef or lamb stew meat, cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup yellow split peas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 dried Persian limes (Limoo Amani) pierced with a fork
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinammon
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 2 tablespoons bloomed saffron
  • 3-4 cups water
  • 2 large potatoes peeled and cut into thin sticks
  • 1/2 cup oil for frying potatoes and cooking

Steps

  1. Soak the yellow split peas in 2 cups of cold water overnight and change the water a few times.
  2. Cut the beef into small chunks.
  3. Chop the onion finely. Add a little oil to the pot and then add chopped onion.
  4. Sauté onion until golden and soft over medium heat. Add turmeric, pepper, and tomato paste.
  5. Sauté tomato paste and onion for 4-5 minutes over medium-high heat.
  6. Add beef to the pot and brown on all sides.
  7. Drain yellow split peas and add in.
  8. Add 3 cups of water, cover the pot and cook the stew for 1 hour.
  9. Cover the pot and bring it to a boil, then turn down the heat, and the stew simmer gently for 1 hour.
  10. Add dried limes and let the stew simmer for another hour.
  11. Add salt, bloomed saffron, and cinnamon to the stew and let it thicken just enough.
  12. Cut potatoes into thin strips.
  13. Rinse potato strips in a colander under cold running water and pat dry potatoes with a clean kitchen towel.
  14. Heat oil in a saucepan. Deep fry the potatoes until golden brown and crispy.
  15. Ladle stew into a bowl. Top with crispy french fries and serve with a plate of fluffy Persian saffron rice.
